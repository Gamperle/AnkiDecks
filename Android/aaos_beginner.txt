# ==========================================
# 3.1 AAOS Overview & Architecture
# ==========================================

What is Android Automotive OS (AAOS)?	A full Android OS embedded directly in the vehicle's head unit (IVI), running natively on the car's hardware. It is NOT a phone projection — the car IS the Android device.
What is the difference between Android Automotive OS and Android Auto?	Android Auto projects a phone's apps onto the car's display via USB/wireless (the phone is the brain). AAOS runs the full Android OS natively in the car hardware — no phone needed.
What is the AAOS system architecture from bottom to top?	Linux Kernel → Vehicle HAL (VHAL) → Car Services → Android Framework + Car APIs → Car Apps (OEM, third-party, Google)
What is an IVI (In-Vehicle Infotainment) system?	The in-car computing unit that provides entertainment (media, radio), navigation, phone connectivity, vehicle settings, and climate control via a touchscreen display
Besides IVI, what other displays can AAOS target?	Digital instrument cluster (gauges, speed, navigation behind steering wheel), rear-seat entertainment displays, and heads-up displays (HUD)
What are Google Automotive Services (GAS)?	Google's proprietary apps and services for AAOS (Google Maps, Google Assistant, Play Store) that OEMs license separately from AOSP. Not all AAOS vehicles include GAS.
Can AAOS run without Google services?	Yes — AAOS is built from AOSP. OEMs can ship vehicles with their own app store, maps, and assistant without licensing Google Automotive Services (e.g., some Chinese OEMs).
What types of apps can run on AAOS?	Media apps, navigation apps, communication apps (calling/messaging), vehicle settings, parking apps, point-of-interest (POI) apps, and video apps (parked only)
What is the Car App Library?	A Jetpack library that provides template-based UI for building apps that work on both Android Auto (phone projection) and Android Automotive OS, ensuring driver distraction compliance
How are AAOS apps distributed?	Via the Play Store's automotive track (for GAS-equipped vehicles), pre-installed by the OEM in the system image, or through OEM-specific app stores
What are driver distraction guidelines in AAOS?	Rules restricting UI complexity while the vehicle is in motion — limiting text length, disabling scrollable lists, blocking video, requiring template-based UIs, and minimizing interactions
What is multi-user support in AAOS?	AAOS supports multiple user profiles (driver, passenger, guest) with separate settings, preferences, and data — the driver profile switches when a different person drives
What is AOSP and how does it relate to AAOS?	AOSP (Android Open Source Project) is the open-source foundation of Android. AAOS is built on top of AOSP with added vehicle-specific components (VHAL, Car Services, Car APIs). OEMs can build AAOS from AOSP without licensing Google services.
What are Car Services in AAOS?	System-level Android services that expose vehicle-specific functionality — CarPropertyService (vehicle data), CarAudioService, CarPowerManagementService, CarUserService, CarInputService, and others. They sit between the Android Framework and the Vehicle HAL.
What is the role of the Vehicle HAL (VHAL) in AAOS?	VHAL is the abstraction layer between Android and the vehicle's hardware/ECUs. It exposes vehicle properties (speed, gear, HVAC settings) as standardized key-value pairs that Car Services and apps can read, write, or subscribe to.
What is a vehicle property in AAOS?	A standardized key-value pair exposed by the VHAL representing a vehicle function (e.g., PERF_VEHICLE_SPEED, HVAC_TEMPERATURE_SET). Each property has an ID, area type (global, seat, window), access mode (read/write), and change mode (on-change, continuous).
What are template-based UIs in the Car App Library?	Pre-defined screen layouts (ListTemplate, GridTemplate, NavigationTemplate, MessageTemplate, etc.) that enforce driver distraction compliance. Apps populate templates with data, but the host renders the final UI to ensure consistent, safe presentation.
What categories of apps are allowed on the Play Store automotive track?	Media (audio), navigation, parking/charging, communication (calling/messaging), point-of-interest (POI), and video (while parked). Apps must meet Google's automotive app quality guidelines and distraction requirements.
What is the AAOS system image and how is it deployed?	The full OS image (kernel, AOSP, VHAL implementation, Car Services, OEM apps, GAS if licensed) flashed onto the head unit at the factory. OTA updates can update the entire system image or individual partitions after deployment.
What happens to apps in AAOS when the vehicle is in motion vs. parked?	When driving, apps face strict distraction restrictions — shorter text, no scrollable lists, no video, limited interactions, template-only UIs. When parked, restrictions relax — video playback is allowed, and richer interactions are permitted.

# ==========================================
# 3.2 Vehicle Hardware Basics
# ==========================================

What is an ECU (Electronic Control Unit)?	A dedicated computer in a vehicle that controls a specific system — engine ECU, transmission ECU, ABS ECU, body control module (BCM), ADAS ECU, infotainment ECU, etc.
How many ECUs does a modern vehicle typically contain?	A modern vehicle contains 70–150+ ECUs, each managing specific functions from engine control to seat adjustment
What is CAN bus (Controller Area Network)?	A robust vehicle bus standard (ISO 11898) that allows ECUs to communicate without a central host. Messages are broadcast with prioritized arbitration based on message IDs.
What is the structure of a CAN message frame?	Arbitration ID (11 or 29 bits, determines priority), DLC (data length code, 0-8 bytes), Data field (0-8 bytes payload), CRC, ACK, and control bits
How does CAN bus arbitration work?	When multiple ECUs transmit simultaneously, dominant bits (0) override recessive bits (1). The ECU with the lowest ID wins arbitration and continues transmitting; others wait and retry.
What is the difference between standard and extended CAN frames?	Standard CAN uses 11-bit identifiers (2048 possible IDs). Extended CAN uses 29-bit identifiers (536 million possible IDs) for larger networks.
What is CAN-FD (Flexible Data-rate)?	An extension of CAN that supports larger payloads (up to 64 bytes vs 8) and higher data rates (up to 8 Mbps vs 1 Mbps in the data phase) while remaining backward-compatible with classic CAN
What is OBD-II?	On-Board Diagnostics II — a standardized diagnostic port (SAE J1962), communication protocols, and parameter IDs (PIDs) that allow external tools to read vehicle data and trouble codes
What are DTCs (Diagnostic Trouble Codes)?	Standardized codes (e.g., P0300 = random/multiple cylinder misfires) stored by ECUs when they detect a fault. Readable via OBD-II scanners or diagnostic tools.
What is LIN bus?	Local Interconnect Network — a low-cost, single-wire serial bus for low-speed, non-critical vehicle functions like seat controls, mirrors, window regulators, and interior lighting
What is FlexRay?	A high-speed (10 Mbps), deterministic, fault-tolerant bus designed for safety-critical applications like drive-by-wire, active suspension, and advanced chassis control
What is Automotive Ethernet (100BASE-T1)?	Single twisted-pair Ethernet for in-vehicle networking, providing 100 Mbps (100BASE-T1) or 1 Gbps (1000BASE-T1) bandwidth for high-data applications like cameras, ADAS, and infotainment updates
What is BroadR-Reach?	A physical layer Ethernet standard developed for automotive that uses a single unshielded twisted pair for full-duplex communication, reducing weight and cost compared to standard Ethernet cables
What is TSN (Time-Sensitive Networking)?	IEEE 802.1 standards that add deterministic, low-latency guarantees to Ethernet — critical for time-sensitive automotive applications like ADAS sensor data, audio synchronization, and control systems
What does a typical IVI head unit SoC contain?	Application processor (CPU cores for Android), GPU (display rendering), DSP (audio processing), connectivity (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth), memory controller, display interfaces, and camera inputs
What are rotary controllers in vehicles?	Physical dial/knob input devices (e.g., BMW iDrive, Audi MMI) used for navigating the infotainment UI without touching the screen, reducing driver distraction
What is a gateway ECU?	A central ECU that routes messages between different vehicle bus domains (powertrain CAN, body CAN, chassis CAN, infotainment Ethernet), acting as a firewall and protocol translator to isolate and protect safety-critical networks from non-critical ones.
What is the difference between CAN and LIN in terms of use cases?	CAN is used for medium- to high-speed, critical communication (engine, transmission, ABS) with multi-master arbitration. LIN is used for low-speed, non-critical peripherals (mirrors, seats, lights) with a single-master/multi-slave topology and lower cost.
What is SOME/IP (Scalable service-Oriented MiddlewarE over IP)?	A middleware protocol for automotive Ethernet that enables service-oriented communication between ECUs — supporting service discovery, RPC (remote procedure calls), and event notifications. Used for ADAS, infotainment, and diagnostics over IP networks.
What is UDS (Unified Diagnostic Services)?	An ISO 14229 standard diagnostic protocol used by ECUs. It defines services like reading/clearing DTCs, reading data by ID, ECU reset, firmware flashing, and security access. It runs over CAN (ISO 15765) or Ethernet (DoIP).
What is DoIP (Diagnostics over Internet Protocol)?	ISO 13400 standard that carries UDS diagnostic communication over Ethernet/TCP-IP instead of CAN, enabling faster data transfer for tasks like firmware updates, large diagnostic data reads, and remote diagnostics.
What is a zonal architecture in vehicles?	A modern vehicle E/E architecture that replaces domain-based ECU clusters with zone controllers — powerful ECUs placed in physical zones of the vehicle (front-left, rear-right, etc.) that consolidate multiple functions, connected via high-speed Ethernet backbone.
What is the purpose of a DSP (Digital Signal Processor) in an IVI head unit?	Handles real-time audio processing tasks — equalization, noise cancellation, surround sound mixing, hands-free call echo cancellation, and audio routing to multiple speaker zones — offloading these from the main CPU.
What is LVDS and why is it used in vehicles?	Low-Voltage Differential Signaling — a high-speed, low-power serial communication standard used to transmit display data from the head unit to screens (instrument cluster, rear-seat displays). It resists electromagnetic interference well in the noisy vehicle environment.
What is a Body Control Module (BCM)?	An ECU that manages body electronics — interior/exterior lighting, door locks, power windows, wipers, horn, and immobilizer. It consolidates many small functions that would otherwise require individual controllers.
